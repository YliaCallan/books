Description

In The Architecture of Silence, Ylia Callan presents a radical synthesis of physics, philosophy, and direct experience to reveal how the fundamental structures of the cosmos — light, geometry, gravity, and information — are not merely external phenomena but active participants in the emergence of consciousness and culture.

Drawing from frontier science, ancient wisdom traditions, and personal exploration in the Australian wilderness, Callan invites readers to see silence not as absence, but as the generative ground from which meaning, music, and mind arise.

This book is both an intellectual adventure and a contemplative guide — a map for those who sense that the universe is not just something we observe, but something that observes through us.

Key Themes

  • Cosmic information as proto-consciousness
  • The role of geometry and resonance in perception
  • Silence as the substrate of creativity
  • Light as memory and carrier of meaning
  • Re-enchantment of science through direct experience
